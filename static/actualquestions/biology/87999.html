Question

Outline the cell theory

[2]
a.

Annotate the electron micrograph of the Escherichia coli cell with the function of the structures labelled I and II.

[2]
b (i).

Calculate the magnification of the electron micrograph. 

[1]
b (ii).

Markscheme

a. living things are composed of cells;
b. cells are the basic/smallest unit of life;
c. cells come from pre-existing cells;
Do not accept cells are the “smallest organisms”.
Do not accept “cells are the building blocks” of life on its own.

a.

a. I: locomotion;
b. II: attachment to surfaces / holds bacteria together / conjugation;
Do not accept “exchange material” on its own.
If more than one function is given, mark the first answer only.

b (i).

×15 000 (accept answers in the range of × 14 000 to × 16 000)

b (ii).

Examiners report

Most gained both marks for their knowledge of cell theory in part a.

 

a.

Students who read the rubric correctly in b scored well. However approximately half of the students read "label" instead of "function".

b (i).

A disappointing number had no idea how to calculate the magnification (15000X) and some answers showed no concept of scale, with answers such as 0.15 X or 5x10-7 X.

b (ii).
