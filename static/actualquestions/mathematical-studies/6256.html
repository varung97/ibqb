Question

The graph of a quadratic function \(y = f (x)\) is given below.

Write down the equation of the axis of symmetry.

[2]
a.

Write down the coordinates of the minimum point.

[2]
b.

Write down the range of \(f (x)\).

[2]
c.

Markscheme

x = 3     (A1)(A1)     (C2)


Notes: Award (A1) for “ x = ” (A1) for 3.

The mark for x = is not awarded unless a constant is seen on the other side of the equation.

 

[2 marks]

a.

(3, −14)  (Accept x = 3, y = −14)     (A1)(ft)(A1)     (C2)


Note: Award (A1)(A0) for missing coordinate brackets.

 

[2 marks]

b.

y ≥ −14     (A1)(A1)(ft)     (C2)


Notes: Award (A1) for y ≥ , (A1)(ft) for –14.

Accept alternative notation for intervals.

 

[2 marks]

c.

Examiners report

The lack of the answer, “ x = 3 ”, expressed as an equation was a common fault.

a.

Misreading the y coordinate was the common error.

b.

This part proved challenging for many; there was confusion between domain and range for many, the incorrect inequality also was a common error. It is the accepted practice in examinations that if a domain is not specified, then it is taken as the real numbers.

c.
