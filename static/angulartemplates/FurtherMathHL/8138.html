Question

\(G\) is a group. The elements \(a,b \in G\) , satisfy \({a^3} = {b^2} = e\) and \(ba = {a^2}b\) , where \(e\) is the identity element of \(G\) .

Show that \({(ba)^2} = e\) .

[3]
a.

Express \({(bab)^{ - 1}}\) in its simplest form.

[3]
b.

Given that \(a \ne e\) ,

  (i)     show that \(b \ne e\) ;

  (ii)     show that \(G\) is not Abelian.

[6]
c.

Markscheme

EITHER

\(baba = ba{a^2}b\)     M1

\( = b{a^3}b\)     (A1)

\( = {b^2}\)     A1

\( = e\)     AG

OR

\(baba = {a^2}bba\)     M1

\( = {a^2}{b^2}a\)      (A1)

\( = {a^3}\)     A1

\( = e\)     AG

[3 marks]

a.

\(bab = {a^2}bb\)     (M1)

\( = {a^2}\)     (A1)

\({(bab)^{ - 1}} = a\)     A1

[3 marks]

b.

(i)     assume \(b = e\)     M1

then \(a = {a^2}\)     A1

\( \Rightarrow a = e\) which is a contradiction     R1

 

(ii)     if \(ab = ba\)     M1

then \(ab = {a^2}b\)     A1

\( \Rightarrow a = e\) which is a contradiction     R1
