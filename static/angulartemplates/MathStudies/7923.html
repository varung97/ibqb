Question

A function is represented by the equation

\[f(x) = a{x^2} + \frac{4}{x} - 3\]

Find \(f ′(x)\) .

[3]
a.

The function \(f (x)\) has a local maximum at the point where \(x = −1\).

Find the value of a.

[3]
b.

Markscheme

\(f(x) = a{x^2} + 4{x^{ - 1}} - 3\)

\(f'(x) = 2ax - 4{x^{ - 2}}\)     (A3)

(A1) for 2ax, (A1) for –4x –2 and (A1) for derivative of –3 being zero.     (C3)

[3 marks]

a.

\(2ax - 4x^{-2} = 0\)     (M1)

\(2a( - 1) - 4{( - 1)^{ - 2}} = 0\)     (M1)

\( -2a - 4 = 0\)

\(a = -2\)     (A1)(ft)
